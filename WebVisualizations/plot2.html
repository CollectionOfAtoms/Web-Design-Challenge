Analysis: Latitude vs. Humidity

The elephant in the room for this plot is that single outlier near the equator This clearly must be an error as humidity can only ever reach 100%.

Ignoring that we can still glean useful insights from the data. One thing that I notice is that the northern hemisphere appears to be much more arid than the southern. I briefly discussed on the main page how the sampling method biased toward island and coastal cities. I think that the large north american and eurasian continents account for this discrepancy, as a random point picked in the northern hemisphere is much more likely to fall on land than one picked in the southern hemisphere. I suspect that humidity has much more to do with a city's proximity to water than anything else. If true, then finding inland cities is simply more likely given our sampling method in the northern hemisphere. If I am mistaken about this, then the data imply that the warmer seasons appear to have a negative effect on the humidity, which I find non-intuitive, as I'd expect solar radiation to vaporise water
